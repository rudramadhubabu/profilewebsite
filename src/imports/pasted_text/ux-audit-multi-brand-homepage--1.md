UX AUDIT — MULTI-BRAND HOMEPAGE ANALYSIS
Case Study Section Title

Understanding the Existing Safety Ecosystem

Subtitle

Before proposing a new UX strategy, I conducted a detailed audit of the existing ecosystem websites to understand how safety professionals currently navigate and discover industrial safety solutions.

The Justrite Safety Group ecosystem includes multiple specialized brand websites:

• Justrite
• Checkers
• NoTrax
• Eagle

Each brand focuses on different safety categories but operates within the same industrial safety market.

However, these platforms currently function as independent experiences rather than a unified ecosystem.

1 — PURPOSE OF THE SITE

The Justrite website focuses on hazardous material storage and workplace chemical safety.

Primary product categories include:

• Safety cabinets
• Chemical storage
• Safety cans
• Spill containment
• Gas cylinder storage
• Safety showers and eyewash stations

The platform targets professionals responsible for workplace safety compliance.

2 — PRIMARY USER GOALS

Users visiting the Justrite homepage typically want to:

• Find compliant chemical storage solutions
• Identify products for hazardous materials
• Understand OSHA or NFPA compliance
• Compare cabinet sizes and specifications
• Request expert consultation

4 — UX STRENGTHS

Clear industrial positioning.

Large product visuals make it easier to recognize equipment.

Strong emphasis on compliance and safety standards.

Industry-specific sections help contextualize products.

Trust signals such as Made in USA build credibility.

5 — UX PAIN POINTS

Product discovery requires multiple navigation steps.

Navigation labels are product-focused rather than problem-focused.

Users must already understand industrial terminology.

Cross-brand discovery is missing.

Example:

A user needing ground protection mats must leave the site and discover the NoTrax brand separately.

Information density in product categories may overwhelm first-time users.

6 — UX OPPORTUNITIES

Introduce hazard-based navigation.

Example:

Instead of product categories:

• flammable liquid storage
• chemical spill prevention
• gas cylinder protection

Introduce guided product selectors.

Allow users to input:

• type of chemical
• volume capacity
• facility environment

Provide cross-brand safety recommendations.

ECOSYSTEM UX INSIGHT SUMMARY

Across the four websites, the audit revealed a common pattern.

Each platform successfully serves its own product category.

However, the overall ecosystem lacks a unified safety discovery experience.

Key problems include:

• fragmented navigation
• brand-based product discovery
• duplicate support features
• disconnected product journeys
