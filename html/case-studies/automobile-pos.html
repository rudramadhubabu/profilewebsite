Automobile Point of Sale System

Streamlining dealership operations with an intuitive, comprehensive POS solution

Project Overview

A comprehensive Point of Sale (POS) system designed for automobile dealerships to streamline vehicle sales, customer management, documentation, and payment processing.

Role

Lead Product Designer

Timeline

6 months (2024-2025)

Platform

Web Application

Team

3 Designers, 8 Engineers

The Problem

Automobile dealerships were struggling with fragmented systems for sales, documentation, and customer management, leading to inefficiencies, errors, and poor customer experiences.

Challenge 1: Fragmented Workflows

Dealership staff had to use multiple disconnected systems for different tasks, causing delays and errors.

Challenge 2: Complex Documentation

Vehicle sales require extensive paperwork, making the process time-consuming and error-prone.

Challenge 3: Payment Processing

Managing multiple payment methods and financing options was cumbersome.

Challenge 4: Customer Management

Tracking customer interactions and maintaining relationships was difficult without a centralized system.

User Research

We conducted extensive research with dealership sales staff, managers, and customers to understand their pain points and needs.

Research Methods

  • 15 in-depth interviews with sales staff
  • 5 contextual inquiries at dealerships
  • Survey of 100+ dealership employees
  • Competitive analysis of 8 POS systems

Key Insights

  • 85% of staff wanted a unified system
  • Average sale took 3-4 hours due to manual processes
  • Documentation errors occurred in 30% of transactions
  • Customers wanted faster, transparent processes

User Personas

Sarah - Sales Representative

Age: 32 | Experience: 5 years

Goals: Close sales quickly, provide excellent customer service, minimize paperwork

Pain Points: Too many systems to learn, slow documentation process, difficulty tracking customer history

Mike - Dealership Manager

Age: 45 | Experience: 15 years

Goals: Increase sales efficiency, reduce errors, monitor team performance, ensure compliance

Pain Points: Lack of real-time visibility, training new staff is difficult, compliance concerns

Information Architecture

We designed a clear, intuitive information architecture that organizes all dealership operations into logical modules.

Main Modules:

  • Dashboard: Overview of sales, inventory, and performance metrics
  • Vehicle Management: Inventory tracking, pricing, specifications
  • Customer Management: CRM, contact history, preferences
  • Sales Process: Quote generation, test drives, negotiation
  • Documentation: Automated form generation, digital signatures
  • Payment Processing: Multiple payment methods, financing integration
  • Reporting: Sales analytics, performance tracking

Key Features

Generate all required forms automatically, with digital signature support and compliance checking.

Usability Testing

We conducted multiple rounds of usability testing to refine the design and ensure it met user needs.

Testing Approach

  • 3 rounds of testing with 20 participants
  • Task-based scenarios covering key workflows
  • A/B testing for critical interfaces
  • Think-aloud protocol to understand user thinking

Key Improvements

  • Simplified navigation reduced task time by 40%
  • Added contextual help for complex features
  • Improved form validation messaging
  • Enhanced mobile touch targets

Key Learnings

  • Involving users early and often was critical to success
  • Simplifying complex processes requires deep domain knowledge
  • Mobile-first thinking improved the overall design
  • Compliance and legal requirements must be baked into the design
  • Training and onboarding are as important as the UI itself
